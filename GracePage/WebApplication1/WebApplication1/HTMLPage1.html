“There’s a foreigner in town!” It was with these opening lines from a student-written song that kick-started Brookfield Central’s Spring Play, The Foreigner. The Bluegrass Band that held the audience’s attention also filled the down-time during intermission and during scene changes, becoming an integral part of the cast. During auditions, Mrs. Schiro, the director of the play, brought together six students for this band. Each of the girls brought their own unique sound with different instruments. “At the first practice, we had some issues. With such a mix of instruments, we had to work to get them to sound right together,” said Riley Swart (‘18), player of the ukulele. This student-run group worked everyday after school. They played six different songs, including one composed and written entirely by the group. These six songs were played on the ukulele, cello, guitar, piano, tambourine, and cabasa. “It was amazing how quickly we got through songs and how much each person contributed to the band! It was so great to still be able to perform with less time commitment,” recalled Vicky Tarakanova (‘17). Even with the small time commitment, they were still able to put on a great show. “The band really stole the show, especially with the theme song they wrote,” said Rae Davel (‘17), who was a townsperson in the play. “It was fun to hear them perform before the show started. It made us more excited backstage,” said Dylan Pashke (‘16) who worked on stage crew for the play. Overall, the BCHS Georgia Hills Bluegrass Band was a success that gave this year’s Spring Play a step above all previous productions. -RILEY SWART
